## 6. Referral Program Framework

### Structure

Once you have 3–5 completed engagements, introduce a formal referral program for CPA firms.

### Recommended Terms

- Referral fee: 10–15% of the advisory fee for each referred engagement that results in a completed credit study
- Paid upon client payment (not upon engagement signing)
- No exclusivity required — firms can refer to other specialists as well
- Referral agreement documented in a simple one-page letter

### How to Position It

Don't lead with the referral fee in initial conversations. Build the relationship first. Once a CPA firm has referred 1–2 clients and seen the quality of work, introduce the referral program as a formalization of the partnership.

Talking points:

"We've been working well together on [client name]'s engagement, and I wanted to formalize our partnership. We offer a referral fee of [X]% for each engagement that results in a completed study. It's our way of recognizing the trust your firm places in us and the value of the introduction."

### Legal Considerations

- Referral fees between advisory firms and CPAs are generally permissible but may be subject to state CPA board rules. Confirm SC-specific rules with legal counsel.
- Document all referral agreements in writing.
- Ensure transparency — clients should know that a referral relationship exists if required by applicable standards.
